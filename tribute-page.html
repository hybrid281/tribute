Dr. Norman Borlaug

The father of the "Green Revolution"

  • He was awarded the Nobel Peach Prize in 1970 for combating world hunger and saving hundreds of millions of lives.
  • Utilized innovative breeding techniques to help create disease-resistant types of wheat, in turn producing more wheat than the standard type.
  • This achievement helped avert famine on a global scale in the second half of the 20th century.
  • "He has probably done more and is known by fewer people than anybody that has done that much," said Dr. Ed Runge, retired head of Texas A&M University's Department of Soil and Crop Sciences
